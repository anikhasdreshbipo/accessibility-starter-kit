# Physical Workspace Accessibility Checklist

Use this checklist to evaluate and improve the physical accessibility of your workplace. Each item should be reviewed periodically and addressed as needed.

## 1. Adjustable Seating & Workstations
- [ ] Height‑adjustable desks (electric or manual) available for all employees
- [ ] Ergonomic chairs with adjustable seat height, depth, backrest, and armrests
- [ ] Footrests provided where desks cannot be lowered enough
- [ ] Monitor arms or stands that allow height, tilt, and distance adjustments
- [ ] Keyboard trays that can be positioned independently of the desk surface
- [ ] Workspace allows both seated and standing work options

## 2. Lighting & Visual Comfort
- [ ] General lighting levels meet recommended standards (≥ 500 lux for office tasks)
- [ ] Task lighting available for individual workstations
- [ ] Glare reduction: matte finishes on screens, anti‑glare filters, blinds on windows
- [ ] Lighting controls accessible from a seated position
- [ ] High‑contrast signage and labels (e.g., dark text on light background)
- [ ] Emergency exit signs are clearly visible and include pictograms

## 3. Pathways & Circulation
- [ ] Minimum clear width of corridors and aisles is 36 inches (91 cm)
- [ ] No obstructions (boxes, cords, furniture) in walkways
- [ ] Non‑slip flooring in all common areas
- [ ] Thresholds are flush (max ¼ inch / 6 mm) or ramped
- [ ] Handrails installed on both sides of stairways where needed
- [ ] Elevators (if present) have Braille labels, audible floor announcements, and controls at reachable height

## 4. Restroom Accessibility
- [ ] At least one wheelchair‑accessible stall with proper door clearance (≥ 32 inches / 81 cm)
- [ ] Grab bars installed beside and behind the toilet
- [ ] Sink height no higher than 34 inches (86 cm) with clear knee space underneath
- [ ] Lever‑style faucet handles (not knobs)
- [ ] Soap dispensers, paper towels, and hand dryers within reach range (15‑48 inches / 38‑122 cm)
- [ ] Emergency call cord or button within easy reach of the toilet and shower (if provided)

## 5. Emergency Egress & Safety
- [ ] Accessible evacuation routes clearly marked on floor plans
- [ ] Visual fire alarms (strobes) installed in addition to audible alarms
- [ ] Emergency instructions available in multiple formats (printed, digital, large print)
- [ ] Designated areas of refuge (if building has phased evacuation)
- [ ] Employees with disabilities are included in emergency drills and have personalized evacuation plans

## 6. Signage & Wayfinding
- [ ] Signage uses high‑contrast colors and simple, sans‑serif fonts
- [ ] Tactile/Braille labels on permanent room signs (e.g., restrooms, meeting rooms)
- [ **] Pictograms used alongside text where appropriate
- [ ] Directional signs placed at consistent heights (approx. 48‑60 inches / 122‑152 cm)

## 7. Assistive Technology & Communication
- [ ] Screen‑reader‑compatible digital signage (if used)
- [ ] Hearing‑loop or FM system installed in meeting rooms and reception areas
- [ ] Amplified telephones available for employees with hearing loss
- [ ] Video‑relay service (VRS) provision for deaf/hard‑of‑hearing employees
- [ ] Communication boards or picture‑based tools available for non‑verbal individuals

## 8. Break & Common Areas
- [ ] Tables with clear knee space (height ≥ 27 inches / 69 cm, depth ≥ 19 inches / 48 cm)
- [ ] Seating options include chairs with and without armrests
- [ ] Vending machines, microwaves, and coffee stations within reach range
- [ ] Quiet zones or low‑stimulation areas available for neurodiverse employees
