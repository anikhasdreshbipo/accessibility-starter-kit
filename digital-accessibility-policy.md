# Digital Accessibility Policy Template

## 1. Purpose & Scope
This policy establishes the requirements for making all digital content, tools, and services accessible to people with disabilities. It applies to:

- Public‑facing websites and web applications
- Internal intranets, portals, and collaboration platforms
- Mobile applications (iOS, Android, etc.)
- Electronic documents (PDFs, Word, Excel, PowerPoint)
- Multimedia (video, audio, podcasts)
- Software tools purchased or developed in‑house
- Third‑party platforms used by employees or customers

## 2. Policy Statement
[Organization Name] is committed to providing an inclusive digital experience for all users, including those with visual, auditory, motor, cognitive, and neurological disabilities. We strive to meet or exceed the following standards:

- **Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA** for all web content
- **Section 508** of the Rehabilitation Act (where applicable)
- **EN 301 549** (European accessibility requirements for ICT products and services)
- **Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA)** (where applicable)

## 3. Roles & Responsibilities

### 3.1 Leadership
- Approve and champion the digital accessibility policy
- Allocate budget and resources for accessibility initiatives
- Ensure accessibility is included in strategic planning

### 3.2 Digital/IT Teams
- Implement accessibility requirements in design, development, and testing
- Conduct regular automated and manual accessibility audits
- Maintain an accessibility‑friendly component library and design system
- Provide training on accessible coding practices

### 3.3 Content Creators & Communications
- Ensure all new content (text, images, video, documents) meets accessibility guidelines
- Add alternative text for images, captions for videos, and transcripts for audio
- Use heading structures, descriptive links, and sufficient color contrast

### 3.4 Procurement & Vendor Management
- Include accessibility requirements in RFPs and vendor contracts
- Evaluate third‑party tools for compliance before purchase
- Require vendors to provide Voluntary Product Accessibility Templates (VPATs)

### 3.5 Human Resources
- Ensure internal HR systems (recruitment, benefits, training) are accessible
- Provide reasonable accommodations for employees using digital tools

## 4. Technical Requirements

### 4.1 Web & Mobile
- Semantic HTML (proper use of headings, lists, landmarks)
- Keyboard navigation and focus management
- Sufficient color contrast (minimum 4.5:1 for normal text)
- Resizable text up to 200% without loss of content or functionality
- Labels, instructions, and error messages that are programmatically associated with form controls
- Captions, transcripts, and audio descriptions for multimedia
- Avoidance of content that flashes more than three times per second

### 4.2 Documents
- Use built‑in styles (Headings, Lists, Tables) in Word, Excel, PowerPoint
- Add alternative text to images and charts
- Ensure reading order is logical in PDFs
- Tag PDFs for accessibility (using Adobe Acrobat Pro or equivalent)
- Provide accessible versions upon request

### 4.3 Software & Tools
- Support screen readers (JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver, TalkBack)
- Compatible with speech‑recognition software (Dragon, Windows Speech Recognition)
- Provide keyboard shortcuts for all major functions
- Allow customization of display (colors, fonts, spacing)

## 5. Training & Awareness
- Mandatory accessibility training for all employees who create or manage digital content
- Role‑specific training for designers, developers, and testers
- Regular updates on accessibility standards and best practices
- Internal accessibility champions program

## 6. Testing & Compliance

### 6.1 Testing Methods
- Automated scanning (e.g., axe, WAVE, Lighthouse)
- Manual keyboard‑only testing
- Screen‑reader testing with JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver
- User testing with people with disabilities

### 6.2 Reporting & Remediation
- Quarterly accessibility audits with findings documented
- Prioritized remediation plan for any identified barriers
- Public accessibility statement (for public‑facing sites) that includes contact information for reporting issues

## 7. Review & Updates
This policy will be reviewed annually and updated as needed to reflect changes in technology, standards, and organizational needs.

## Appendix A: Quick Reference Checklist
- [ ] All images have descriptive alternative text
- [ ] Videos have captions and transcripts
- [ ] Color is not the only means of conveying information
- [ ] All functionality is operable via keyboard
- [ ] Form fields have visible labels and clear error messages
- [ ] Text can be resized up to 200% without breaking layout
- [ ] Headings are used in a logical order (H1 → H2 → H3)
- [ ] Links are descriptive (avoid “click here”)
- [ ] Tables have header cells identified
- [ ] No content flashes more than three times per second
